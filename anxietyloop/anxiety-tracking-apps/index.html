Best Anxiety Tracking Apps 2026

We tested popular anxiety and mood tracking apps to help you find the right tool for understanding your mental wellness patterns.

Last updated: January 2026

Tracking anxiety can help you notice patterns, identify triggers, and feel more in control. But with dozens of mental health apps available, which ones actually help? We tested the most popular options and ranked them based on simplicity, privacy, and effectiveness.

Important: These apps are wellness tools, not replacements for professional mental health care. If you're struggling with anxiety, please reach out to a mental health professional.

Top 7 Anxiety Tracking Apps

1

Anxiety Loop

Simplest & Most Private

The most focused anxiety tracker available. One tap to check in, see patterns over time, and access breathing exercises when you need them. All data stays on your device — no account, no cloud, no tracking.

Pros

  • 2-second check-ins
  • 100% on-device privacy
  • Built-in breathing exercises
  • No ads, ever
  • Free tracking, $19.99 Pro for patterns + exercises

Cons

  • iOS only
  • No cloud sync/backup
  • Tracks only anxiety (intentional)
Price: Free / $19.99 Pro Platform: iOS Best for: Simple, private anxiety tracking
2

Daylio

The most popular mood tracking app. Tracks mood levels plus activities, helping you find correlations between what you do and how you feel. Great for comprehensive life journaling.

Pros

  • Tracks mood + activities
  • Beautiful statistics
  • Customizable
  • Large community

Cons

  • Ads in free version
  • Takes longer to log
  • Not anxiety-specific
Price: Free / $9.99/year Premium Platform: iOS, Android Best for: Comprehensive mood journaling
3

Bearable

The most comprehensive health tracker. Designed for chronic conditions, it tracks mood, symptoms, medications, and dozens of factors to find correlations. Overkill for simple anxiety tracking but powerful for complex health needs.

Pros

  • Tracks everything
  • Advanced correlations
  • Medication tracking
  • Doctor-ready reports

Cons

  • Complex, time-consuming
  • Expensive premium
  • Steep learning curve
Price: Free / $49.99/year Premium Platform: iOS, Android Best for: Chronic illness & comprehensive tracking
4

Calm

More meditation than tracking, but includes mood check-ins alongside guided meditations, sleep stories, and breathing exercises. Great if you want content alongside tracking.

Pros

  • Excellent meditation content
  • Sleep stories
  • Daily Calm feature
  • Polished experience

Cons

  • Expensive ($70/year)
  • Tracking is secondary
  • Content-focused, not tracking-focused
Price: $69.99/year Platform: iOS, Android, Web Best for: Meditation + light tracking
5

Headspace

Another meditation-first app with mood tracking features. Includes anxiety-specific meditations and SOS sessions for panic moments. Good pairing with a dedicated tracker.

Pros

  • Anxiety-specific content
  • SOS panic sessions
  • Structured courses
  • Scientific backing

Cons

  • Expensive subscription
  • Limited tracking features
  • Content over data
Price: $69.99/year Platform: iOS, Android Best for: Guided anxiety meditation
6

Finch

A unique approach: you care for a virtual pet bird while building self-care habits. Includes mood tracking, goal setting, and journaling. Gamification that actually works for some people.

Pros

  • Engaging gamification
  • Gentle, non-judgmental
  • Good for building habits
  • Generous free tier

Cons

  • Cutesy aesthetic not for everyone
  • Tracking is secondary to game
  • Less analytical
Price: Free / $59.99/year Plus Platform: iOS, Android Best for: Gamified self-care building
7

How We Feel

Developed with Yale scientists, focuses on emotional granularity — naming exactly how you feel beyond "good" or "bad." Free and research-backed.

Pros

  • Completely free
  • Research-backed approach
  • Expands emotional vocabulary
  • Clean design

Cons

  • Not anxiety-specific
  • Takes time to log emotions
  • Limited features
Price: Free Platform: iOS, Android Best for: Understanding emotions deeply

The Bottom Line

For pure anxiety tracking: Anxiety Loop is the simplest, most private option. One tap, done.

For comprehensive mood journaling: Daylio remains the gold standard for tracking mood + activities.

For chronic conditions: Bearable offers the deepest health tracking available.

For meditation + tracking: Calm or Headspace pair content with light tracking.

Most apps offer free tiers — try a few to see what fits your tracking style. The best app is the one you'll actually use consistently.
