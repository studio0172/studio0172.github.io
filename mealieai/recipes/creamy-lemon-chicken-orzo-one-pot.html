Creamy Lemon Chicken Orzo (One Pot)

A bright, weeknight-friendly one-pot meal with tender chicken, silky orzo, and a lemony Parmesan finish. Great for meal prep because it reheats without drying out.

üìù Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dry orzo
  • 2 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 1/3 cup grated Parmesan
  • 1/4 cup half-and-half
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Red pepper flakes, to taste (optional)

Instructions

1

Get your skillet hot and shimmery with olive oil. Season the chicken with salt and pepper, then toss those bite-sized pieces in. Sizzle them for 4‚Äì5 minutes until they turn a lovely golden brown and smell amazing.

2

Add the diced onion straight into the skillet, cook for 2 minutes until they soften and become translucent. Then, stir in the minced garlic and Italian seasoning and let it mingle with everything for 30 seconds of flavor fireworks.

3

Pour in the dry orzo and the chicken broth, then crank the heat up to bring it all to a lively simmer. Once bubbling, reduce the heat to medium-low, slap on a cover, and cook for 10‚Äì12 minutes. Give it a good stir once or twice until the orzo is tender and soaking up all those tasty juices.

4

Toss in the baby spinach to let it wilt into green goodness. Turn off the heat (no more boiling magic needed!). Stir in the Parmesan, half-and-half, and lemon zest and juice. Adjust the seasoning if needed, dig in, and enjoy your creamy, zesty, veggie-packed masterpiece!

Recipe Notes & Tips

  • For extra veg, stir in 1 cup frozen peas during the last 2 minutes of cooking.
  • If it thickens too much after chilling, reheat with a splash of broth or water.
