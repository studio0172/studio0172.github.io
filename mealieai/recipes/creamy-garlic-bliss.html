Creamy Garlic Bliss

This creamy garlic pasta is a flavor-packed bowl of happiness! Rich, velvety, and bursting with garlic goodness, it makes dinner unforgettable. Perfect for when you want comfort food with a fancy twist!

Pasta & Dairy
  • 300g tagliatelle (or any long pasta)
  • 20g salted butter
  • 200ml double cream
  • 85g Parmesan, grated
  • 150ml chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
Vegetables & Herbs
  • 6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 20g flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
Seasonings
  • Black pepper (about 25 twists)

Instructions

1

Cook pasta & start the sauce

Start by boiling the pasta as per the instructions on the box. Meanwhile, get your largest frying pan and melt that glorious salted butter until it's foaming. Add the finely chopped garlic and give it 25 lively twists of your black pepper mill. Cook this garlic-pepper symphony for 4-5 minutes until it's light golden and smelling heavenly.

2

Build the creamy sauce

Now, scoop in the flour and stir it in to create a silky paste. Keep stirring for 1-2 minutes, then slowly pour in the chicken stock while whisking like a boss, until the sauce is smooth and luscious. Pour in the double cream and sprinkle most of the grated Parmesan. Stir until everything is melted, creamy, and combined. Season to taste!

3

Combine pasta & sauce

Grab your drained pasta and transfer it straight into this creamy wonderland, along with a good splash of the cooking water. Mix it all up thoroughly, adding a little more pasta water if the sauce needs to loosen up a bit. Keep stirring until the sauce is thick, glossy, and clinging to every strand of pasta.

4

Serve & enjoy

Divide the pasta into four bowls and top generously with the chopped parsley and the remaining Parmesan. Dive in and enjoy this creamy, garlicky flavor explosion!

Recipe Notes & Tips

  • Pasta choice: Tagliatelle is ideal, but fettuccine, pappardelle, or even spaghetti work great too.
  • Save that pasta water: The starchy water is liquid gold for making your sauce silky and helping it cling to the pasta.
