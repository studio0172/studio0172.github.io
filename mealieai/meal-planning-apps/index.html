Best Meal Planning Apps 2026

We tested the top meal planning apps to help you find the perfect fit for your kitchen routine, budget, and cooking style.

Last updated: January 2026

Meal planning apps can save you hours every week — no more standing in front of the fridge wondering what to cook. But with dozens of options, which one is actually worth your time? We tested the most popular meal planning apps in 2026 and ranked them based on features, ease of use, and value.

Top 7 Meal Planning Apps

1

Mealie AI

Editor's Choice

The newest entry in meal planning, Mealie AI brings artificial intelligence to your kitchen. Ask it what to cook with ingredients you have, get instant nutrition info, and import recipes from any website with AI-powered structuring.

Pros

  • AI assistant for recipe suggestions
  • Import from any website
  • Automatic nutrition calculation
  • Beautiful, modern interface
  • Web app + iOS

Cons

  • Subscription-based pricing
  • No Android app yet
  • Newer, less recipe content built-in
Price: Free tier / $6.99/mo Premium Platforms: iOS, Web Best for: AI-powered planning
2

Mealime

Mealime is perfect for busy people who want quick, healthy meals. Their curated recipe library focuses on dishes under 30 minutes, and the dietary filtering is excellent for special diets.

Pros

  • Excellent dietary filters
  • Quick recipes (under 30 min)
  • Clean, simple interface
  • Good grocery list organization

Cons

  • No AI features
  • Can't import your own recipes
  • Limited to curated library
Price: Free / $5.99/mo Pro Platforms: iOS, Android Best for: Quick healthy meals
3

Paprika Recipe Manager

The classic recipe manager that's been around since 2010. Paprika is rock-solid for saving and organizing recipes with a one-time purchase model that appeals to subscription-fatigued users.

Pros

  • One-time purchase
  • Excellent recipe import
  • Native apps for all platforms
  • Offline-first

Cons

  • Pay separately per platform
  • No AI features
  • Interface feels dated
Price: $4.99 one-time (per platform) Platforms: iOS, Android, Mac, Windows Best for: Recipe organization
4

Yummly

Yummly offers personalized recipe recommendations based on your taste preferences. It's backed by Whirlpool and integrates with their smart appliances.

Pros

  • Huge recipe database
  • Personalized recommendations
  • Step-by-step video guides
  • Smart appliance integration

Cons

  • Ads in free version
  • Recipes link to external sites
  • Can feel cluttered
Price: Free / $4.99/mo Pro Platforms: iOS, Android, Web Best for: Recipe discovery
5

Plan to Eat

A straightforward meal planner with a focus on family planning. Plan to Eat excels at drag-and-drop meal scheduling and has one of the best recipe clippers.

Pros

  • Excellent drag-and-drop planner
  • Great recipe clipper
  • Family sharing
  • Recipe scaling

Cons

  • No free tier
  • Interface looks dated
  • Mobile apps need improvement
Price: $5.95/mo or $49/year Platforms: Web, iOS, Android Best for: Family meal planning
6

Eat This Much

The most nutrition-focused meal planner. Eat This Much automatically generates meal plans based on your calorie and macro targets. Great for fitness enthusiasts.

Pros

  • Automatic plan generation
  • Detailed nutrition tracking
  • Macro customization
  • Fitness app integrations

Cons

  • Expensive premium tier
  • Generated meals can be bland
  • Less flexibility
Price: Free / $9/mo Premium Platforms: Web, iOS, Android Best for: Nutrition/fitness focus
7

AnyList

Primarily a grocery list app, AnyList has grown to include meal planning and recipe features. Great if you want one app for lists and planning.

Pros

  • Best-in-class grocery lists
  • Family sharing
  • Alexa integration
  • Clean design

Cons

  • Meal planning is basic
  • Apple-only for full features
  • Recipe features are secondary
Price: Free / $12.99/year Complete Platforms: iOS, Mac, Web, Android (limited) Best for: Grocery list + planning combo

The Bottom Line

For AI-powered help: Mealie AI is the clear winner if you want intelligent recipe suggestions and automatic nutrition info.

For quick healthy meals: Mealime's curated library and dietary filters are unmatched.

For recipe collectors: Paprika remains the gold standard for organizing your recipe collection.

For fitness goals: Eat This Much generates nutrition-optimized plans automatically.

Most apps offer free trials — we recommend trying 2-3 to see which workflow fits your cooking style.
