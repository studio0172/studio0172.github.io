Who Each App Is Best For

Choose ThinkUp if you:

Our Honest Take

ThinkUp pioneered the "record your own affirmations" approach, and if that resonates with you, it's still one of the best options. Hearing your own voice saying positive things does create a unique experience. The music integration is polished, and the listening experience is well-designed.

That said, Resonant offers a completely different experience at a fraction of the cost. If you prefer reading over listening, want personalization without effort, or simply don't want to record yourself, Resonant is the better fit.

Some users actually use both: ThinkUp for audio during commutes, Resonant for quiet morning reflection. They're complementary rather than competitive.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the main difference between Resonant and ThinkUp?

Resonant offers curated affirmations that learn your preferences automatically, while ThinkUp focuses on recording affirmations in your own voice with background music. Resonant is text-based and passive; ThinkUp is audio-based and requires active recording.

Is Resonant more expensive than ThinkUp?

ThinkUp Premium costs around $12.99/month or $99.99/year, while Resonant Premium is approximately $4.99/month. Resonant is significantly more affordable, and its free tier offers more features.

Which app has better personalization?

Resonant uses intelligent personalization that learns from your behavior over time. ThinkUp is personalized through your own voice recordings but doesn't have adaptive learning. Resonant's approach is more hands-off; ThinkUp requires more upfront effort.

Can I use both apps together?

Yes! Some people use ThinkUp for audio affirmations during commutes and Resonant for quiet reading moments. They complement each other well if you want both audio and text-based practices.
